MED2022 - Foundations for Practice 2

Welcome!

Welcome back to medical school and Year 2 of curriculum 25. The team are looking forward to meeting you again over the next few months and continuing your education towards becoming tomorrow’s doctors. We look forward to teaching you across three units: Gastrointestinal, Renal, Endocrine and Reproductive Systems (GERR), Neurological System and Musculoskeletal System Part 2 (MSK2) with Family Medicine, Clinical Skills and the Student Selected Components and hope you will have an exciting and informative year.

We wish you good luck in the next part of your medical school journey.

How Do I Get Started with Canvas?

If you need a refresher on Canvas, try the Student Canvas Orientation course.

Teaching for Year 2 is organised into 3 units:

  • Gastrointestinal, Renal, Endocrine and Reproductive Systems
  • Neurological System
  • Musculoskeletal System (Part 2)

When you open the Modules link on the left menu bar, you will see the all of the "modules" contained within the Canvas course. These have been categorised into medical themes. These themes will look empty to start with, but will gradually fill as your course content is published throughout the year. All of the relevant links to the lecture, tutorial, CBL session and practical materials are contained here.

At the top of the modules page, you will find a Teaching Materials section listing all of your teaching sessions in chronological order (split into the teaching units), and case overview information in the Case-Based Learning section, which is split into 2-week teaching blocks.

We recommend that you access and download any of the teaching material in advance, and save it to your computer to allow you to access it during the teaching sessions. 

To view all QUB modules on which you are currently enrolled within Canvas, return to the dashboard by clicking the Dashboard link on the navigation bar.

Please contact the disability adviser, Mr Robin Baker (r.baker@qub.ac.uk), to report any accessibility issues with teaching materials or the wider learning experience.
